**Subject:** Your AI ranks a circular above a Court of Cassation ruling. Here's why.

**Preview:** Semantic similarity isn't legal authority. And the difference matters more than you think.

---

Most legal AI systems rank search results by one criterion: semantic similarity. The document that uses the most similar language to your query appears first.

For legal research, this is a structural failure. A circular can use identical language to the statute it interprets — while carrying zero binding force.

We wrote about how authority ranking changes this: weighting search results by their position in the Belgian hierarchy of norms, not just by textual similarity.
