# Email snippet — TR04: temporal versioning

**Subject:** The Belgian corporate tax rate is 25%. It's also 29.58%. Both are correct.

**Preview text:** If your AI tool can't tell 2019 from 2026, its answer is for the wrong year.

---

The Belgian corporate tax rate changed three times in seven years: 33.99% to 29.58% to 25%. Ask a generic AI "what's the rate?" and you get one number — with no indication of which year it applies to.

This is the temporal versioning problem. Belgian tax law changes at least twice a year through program laws. The introduction of a 10% capital gains tax on January 1, 2026 creates the most significant temporal boundary in recent Belgian fiscal history — and any system that can't distinguish "before 2026" from "after 2026" will miscalculate.

We wrote up what temporal versioning requires architecturally — and why most AI tools don't offer it.
