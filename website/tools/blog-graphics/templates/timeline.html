Belgian tax AI: key milestones
2023
ChatGPT enters tax advisory
General-purpose LLMs first used for Belgian tax questions — high hallucination rates, no source citations.
2024
Stanford hallucination study
Dahl et al. find 58–88% hallucination rates in general LLMs vs. 17–33% in specialized legal AI tools.
2025
RAG-based legal tools mature
Retrieval-augmented generation becomes the standard architecture for professional legal AI research.
2026
Auryth TX launches
Purpose-built Belgian tax research with source citations, confidence scoring, and temporal versioning.
